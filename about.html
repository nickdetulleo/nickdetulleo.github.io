About This Project

The vibrant and colorful murals that adorn the walls of Philadelphia are not just a source of aesthetic pleasure, but they also tell a story of the city's cultural and social history. The mural-making tradition in Philadelphia dates back to the early 20th century, but it gained momentum in the 1980s as a response to the city's growing urban blight and the need to revitalize neglected neighborhoods.
